Do you remember the good old days? Back when your password could just be `password123` or your cat's name? Life was simple. The birds were singing. The internet was a happy place.

Those days are gone.

Today, signing up for a new account feels less like creating a profile and more like launching nuclear codes. You just want to order a pizza, but the website demands a security clearance level higher than the President.

Here is the tragic reality of modern digital security.

You type in your usual password. The website laughs at you. A red box appears with a list of demands that looks like a spell from Harry Potter:

* [ ] Must be at least 12 characters long.
* [ ] Must contain at least one uppercase letter (A-Z).
* [ ] Must contain at least one lowercase letter (a-z).
* [ ] Must contain at least one number (0-9).
* [ ] Must contain at least one special character (!@#$%^&*).
* [ ] Must not contain your name, birth year, or the word "password".
* [ ] **(Optional)** Must include the blood of a dragon and the maiden name of your great-grandmother.

## üîÑ The "Reset Password" Paradox

This is the moment that truly breaks a human spirit.

1.  You try to log in.
2.  **"Incorrect Password."**
3.  You try again.
4.  **"Incorrect Password."**
5.  Fine. You click **"Forgot Password?"**

You go to your email, click the link, and type in a new password. You decide to use the one you *thought* was your old password, just to be safe.

**Website Error Message:**
> *"New password cannot be the same as your old password."*

**ME:** EXCUSE ME? IF THAT WAS MY OLD PASSWORD, WHY DIDN'T YOU LET ME LOG IN 5 MINUTES AGO?!

This is the closest I have ever come to throwing my laptop out the window.

## üìù The Sticky Note Solution

Cybersecurity experts say: *"Never write your passwords down."*

Also Cybersecurity experts: *"Make your password impossible to memorize."*

So what do we do? We write it on a yellow sticky note and stick it right on the monitor. Or worse, we save it in a Word document named `passwords.docx` right on the desktop.

We have come full circle. The password is unbreakable, but the key is under the doormat.
